How Valium Works

Valium, also known as diazepam and made by Martin Dow Pharmaceuticals, increases the effects of a neurotransmitter called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain. GABA is like a natural “calm down” signal that helps reduce brain activity.

When you take Valium, it helps GABA work better by attaching to special spots called receptors in the brain. This attachment lets more chloride ions enter brain cells, making them less likely to get over-excited and fire off signals. Essentially, Valium helps calm down the brain’s activity.

Because of this calming effect, Valium medicine is used to treat conditions like anxiety disorders, muscle spasms, and sometimes seizures. It can also make people feel relaxed and sleepy, so it’s sometimes used before medical procedures.

However, Valium should be used carefully and for short periods because it can be habit-forming. Stopping it suddenly can lead to withdrawal symptoms, so doctors often taper the dosage gradually when someone needs to stop taking it.

Side Effects and Precautions

While Valium medicine is effective in treating anxiety and related disorders, it is important to be aware of potential side effects and take necessary precautions:

  • Common Side Effects: Drowsiness, dizziness, blurred vision, and impaired coordination are common side effects of Valium medicine. These effects may impair cognitive and motor functions, necessitating caution when operating machinery or driving.
  • Dependency Risk: Prolonged use of Valium medicine can lead to physical dependence and tolerance. Abrupt discontinuation may result in withdrawal symptoms such as rebound anxiety, tremors, and insomnia. Tapering off Valium medicine under medical supervision is recommended to minimize withdrawal effects.
  • Precautions: Valium prescription should be used cautiously in individuals with a history of substance abuse, respiratory disorders, or liver disease. Dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly patients and those with impaired liver function to reduce the risk of adverse effects.
